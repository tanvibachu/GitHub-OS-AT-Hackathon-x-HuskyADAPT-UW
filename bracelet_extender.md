## Instructions

1. You will use 3 silicone bracelets total. The bracelets have evenly spaced holes along their length, and white plastic connectors on them that can be popped apart (using a small flat tool, like a fingernail or flathead screwdriver) and removed, then re-connected through any hole in the wristband. There are two types of connectors: sockets (recieving part, with a wider opening and large raised inner ring) and studs (inserting part, with a smaller and taller raised ring in the middle). One one end of the bracelet, there will be three socket connectors next to each other. The other end of the bracelet will have a single stud connector.
2. On the first band, move one of the 3 socket connectors next to the stud clasp on the opposite end. You will want both connector pieces facing the same side of the band (both stud and socket on same side of the band).
3. Cut the band so that there is a small piece with just the stud and socket connectors on it, leaving the rest of the first band unused. This small piece is the "bridge".
4. Join the two other intact bands, using the "bridge" piece. The stud on the bridge will connect to the socket closest to the edge on one bracelet, and the socket on the bridge will connect to the stud on the other bracelet.
5. Move a socket connector on the band that has its stud attached to the bridge towards the middle of the band to change the fit. When you reattach the socket piece, you will want it facing the same side of the bracelet as the stud connector that's attached to the bridge (should be on the opposite side from the original sockets)
6. Join the long bands together, and cut off any excess silicon and the two remaining socket connectors.
